Banana Oat Pancakes

6-12 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • old fashioned oats
  • 2 eggs
  • cinnamon powder
  • splash of milk
  • tsp baking powder
  • vanilla extract
  • maple syrup

Recipe Time:

  • prep time: 10 mins
  • cook time: 10 mins
  • total time: 20 mins

This recipe is a healthier alternative to pancake mixes and has all the nutrients of a bowl of
oatmeal with fresh bananas! This recipe has been a staple for me the past few months as I try
to make healthier food choices. It can be modified with so many different mix-ins, ingredients,
flavors, and toppings. In the fall, I like to add canned pumpkin to the batter and top it with
pumpkin spice toppings, brown sugar, and pecans. Pure old fashioned oats can also be used to
make the recipe 100% gluten free!

Directions:

  1. Add all ingredients into a bowl, mash the bananas, and keep adding in oats until thick. For a
    fluffier consistency, the ingredients can be mixed in a blender, but a fork works just fine
    if you don’t own a blender.
  2. Heat and grease pancake griddle or skillet.
  3. Scoop ¼ cup of pancake batter onto the griddle and cook for 2 minutes or until small bubbles
    form and edges are set. Flip the pancake and cook until they are golden brown on both sides.
    Repeat with the rest of the batter.
  4. Add maple syrup, and any topping of your choosing! I like to add brown sugar and pumpkin
    spice topping, as well as fresh strawberries and bananas.
